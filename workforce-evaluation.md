## Methodology

### Scoring Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **AI Replaceability (AIR)** | 0.35 | How much of this role can be performed by AI agents today or within 12 months. 10 = fully replaceable, 1 = impossible to replace. |
| **Human Capital Value (HCV)** | 0.30 | Institutional knowledge, relationships, judgment, leadership, and unique skills this person brings. 10 = irreplaceable human value, 1 = commodity skill set. |
| **Adaptability Potential (AP)** | 0.20 | Likelihood this person can evolve into an AI-augmented role and multiply output 4-6x. 10 = highly adaptable, 1 = rigid/unable to adapt. |
| **Cost-to-Value Ratio (CTV)** | 0.15 | Value delivered relative to compensation. 10 = exceptional value per dollar, 1 = severely overpaid for output. |

### Restructuring Index Formula

```
RI = (AIR x 0.35) + (HCV x 0.30) + (AP x 0.20) + (CTV x 0.15)
```

**Note on interpretation:** A high RI is not inherently good or bad — it depends on which dimensions are driving it. High AIR pulls toward exit; high HCV pulls toward protect. The classification system below accounts for the dimensional profile, not just the composite score.

### Classifications

| Symbol | Classification | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Red | **Exit** | High replaceability (AIR 7+), Low human capital (HCV 1-4), Low adaptability (AP 1-4) |
| Yellow | **Restructure** | Moderate replaceability (AIR 4-6), or mixed scores across dimensions |
| Green | **Retain & Evolve** | Low-moderate replaceability, High human capital or adaptability |
| Star | **Protect** | High human capital (HCV 8+), Strong adaptability, Strong value |
